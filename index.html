سحر In Urdu, Seher means the first light of dawn — the moment darkness gives way to possibility. That is who we are for: young people ready to rise.

Who We Are

A movement led by refugee youth, for refugee youth.

SEHER — Strength, Empowerment & Hope for Emerging Refugees — was founded by Areej Khan out of lived experience. Many refugee youth in Malaysia face limited access to education, restrictions on work, and few spaces to find mentorship or belonging.

We exist to fill those gaps: a safe space where youth connect, learn, and grow together — and step into roles as leaders, advocates, and changemakers. Because we are more than our struggles. We are the dawn of a brighter tomorrow.

Our Pillars

The values that carry us forward

Strength

Building resilience and confidence — because our struggles don't define us. Our resilience does.

Empowerment

Education, leadership skills, and advocacy training that help young people break through barriers.

Hope

Creating real opportunities for a brighter, dignified future — and a true sense of belonging.

Our Mission

To unite refugee youth in Malaysia in a safe space where we find strength in each other, empower ourselves through education, skills and advocacy, and turn our hopes into reality — reclaiming our voices and the confidence to shape our own futures.

Our Vision

A future where refugee youth are not just seen but heard, not just surviving but thriving — with the strength to stand tall, the empowerment to break barriers, and the hope to build a life of dignity and purpose.

What We Do

Our Programs

Practical initiatives that meet youth where they are — and help them rise.

01

Education & Capacity Building

Connecting youth to scholarships, skills workshops, and career counselling so they can make informed choices about their future.

02

Peer Support & Engagement

A safe space to share, community events to connect, and encouragement to take active roles in decision-making.

03

Advocacy & Awareness

Raising awareness of refugee rights, amplifying youth voices, and building stronger relationships with stakeholders.

04

Well-being & Support

Mental-health support through counselling and peer discussion, medical case management, and safe, legal job pathways.

05

Waqar-e-Amal

The dignity of labour. Youth give back through cleaning, beautification and community-service projects — showing their positive contribution to society.

Who We Serve

For the youth carrying tomorrow on their shoulders

Refugee youth aged 15 to 35 across Kuala Lumpur and Selangor — young men and women alike, with a special commitment to lifting young women into leadership.

  • Limited access to education
  • Legal & employment restrictions
  • Few supportive peer networks
  • Uncertainty about the future

SEHER is here to fill these gaps — with safe space, mentorship, and real opportunity.
